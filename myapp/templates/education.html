  1. Malaria

    2. •Causes:
    Caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted through bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. Poor sanitation and stagnant water promote mosquito breeding.

    •Symptoms:
    High fever, chills, sweating, headache, muscle pain, vomiting, and fatigue. Severe cases may lead to anemia or organ failure

    •Prevention:
    High fever, chills, sweating, headache, muscle pain, vomiting, and fatigue. Severe cases may lead to anemia or organ failure

  2. Typhoid Fever

    3. •Causes:
    Infection by Salmonella typhi bacteria through contaminated food or water. Poor hygiene and sanitation are major factors.

    •Symptoms:
    High fever, weakness, stomach pain, headache, constipation or diarrhea, and loss of appetite.

    •Prevention:
    Drink clean or boiled water, wash hands thoroughly, eat properly cooked food, and get the typhoid vaccine.

  3. Cholera

    4. •Causes:
    Infection by Vibrio cholerae bacteria found in contaminated water or food. Common in areas with poor sanitation.

    •Symptoms:
    Profuse watery diarrhea, vomiting, leg cramps, and dehydration which can lead to death if untreated.

    •Prevention:
    Drink safe water, wash fruits and vegetables, practice hand hygiene, and improve sanitation facilities.

  4. Tuberculosis (TB)

    5. •Causes:
    Caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria, spread through droplets from coughing or sneezing..

    •Symptoms:
    Persistent cough (lasting over 3 weeks), chest pain, fever, night sweats, and weight loss.

    •Prevention:
    BCG vaccination, early diagnosis and treatment, and good ventilation in living areas.

  5. Pneumonia

    6. •Causes:
    Infection by bacteria,Streptococcus pneumoniae, viruses, or fungi that inflame lung tissue.

    •Symptoms:
    Cough with phlegm, fever, chills, shortness of breath, and chest pain.

    •Prevention:
    Get vaccinated, avoid smoking, practice hand hygiene, and treat respiratory infections promptly.

  6. Dengue Fever

    7. •Causes:
    Caused by dengue virus transmitted through Aedes mosquitoes.

    •Symptoms:
    Sudden high fever, severe headache, pain behind eyes, joint and muscle pain, skin rash, and bleeding gums.

    •Prevention:
    Use mosquito repellents, wear long sleeves, cover water containers, and prevent mosquito breeding.

  7. COVID-19

    8. •Causes:
    Caused by SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus, transmitted via droplets, surfaces, and air in close spaces.

    •Symptoms:
    Fever, dry cough, tiredness, sore throat, loss of taste or smell, difficulty breathing in severe cases.

    •Prevention:
    Get vaccinated, wear masks, maintain social distance, wash hands regularly, and stay home when ill.

  8. Hepatitis B

    9. •Causes:
    Caused by the Hepatitis B virus, transmitted through blood, sexual contact, or from mother to child during birth.

    •Symptoms:
    Jaundice (yellow eyes/skin), fatigue, loss of appetite, dark urine, abdominal pain.

    •Prevention:
    Get vaccinated, avoid sharing needles or razors, and practice safe sex.

  9. HIV/AIDS

    10. •Causes:
    Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) attacks the immune system. Spread through sexual contact, contaminated needles, or mother-to-child transmission.

    •Symptoms:
    Weight loss, chronic fatigue, fever, night sweats, frequent infections.

    •Prevention:
    Use condoms, get tested regularly, avoid sharing needles, and ensure blood transfusions are screened.

  10. Measles

    11. •Causes:
    Measles virus, spread through droplets from coughs or sneezes.

    •Symptoms:
    Fever, cough, runny nose, red eyes, followed by a rash.

    •Prevention:
    MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) vaccination and good hygiene practices.

  11. Meningitis

    12. •Causes:
    Infection of membranes around the brain and spinal cord by bacteria or viruses.

    •Symptoms:
    Stiff neck, headache, high fever, nausea, vomiting, sensitivity to light, confusion.

    •Prevention:
    Vaccination, avoiding close contact with infected people, and prompt antibiotic treatment.

  12. Anemia

    13. •Causes:
    Iron deficiency, blood loss, or chronic disease.

    •Symptoms:
    Pale skin, fatigue, dizziness, shortness of breath, irregular heartbeat.

    •Prevention:
    Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, beans, red meat), take supplements, and treat underlying causes.

  13. Peptic Ulcer

    14. •Causes:
    Helicobacter pylori infection, excessive acid, long-term use of painkillers, or stress.

    •Symptoms:
    Burning stomach pain, nausea, bloating, and heartburn.

    •Prevention:
    Avoid NSAIDs, reduce alcohol, manage stress, eat balanced meals.

  14. Asthma

    15. •Causes:
    Inflammation of airways triggered by dust, smoke, cold air, or allergens.

    •Symptoms:
    Wheezing, shortness of breath, coughing, tightness in chest.

    •Prevention:
    Avoid triggers, use prescribed inhalers, keep indoor air clean.

  15. Kidney Disease

    16. •Causes:
    Diabetes, hypertension, infections, or prolonged use of painkillers.

    •Symptoms:
    Swelling in legs, fatigue, dark urine, reduced urination.

    •Prevention:
    Drink enough water, manage blood pressure and blood sugar, avoid excessive salt.

  16. Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

    17. •Causes:
    Long-term smoking, air pollution, chemical exposure.

    •Symptoms:
    Persistent cough, mucus production, wheezing, shortness of breath..

    •Prevention:
    Stop smoking, avoid polluted air, wear masks at work, and exercise the lungs through breathing exercises.

  17. liver Cirrhosis

    18. •Causes:
    Long-term alcohol use, hepatitis B/C, or fatty liver.

    •Symptoms:
    Jaundice, fatigue, swollen legs, easy bruising.

    •Prevention:
    limit alcohol, get vaccinated for hepatitis, maintain healthy weight.

  18. Osteoporosis

    19. •Causes:
    Low calcium and vitamin D, hormonal changes, sedentary habits.

    •Symptoms:
    Fragile bones, back pain, frequent fractures.

    •Prevention:
    Eat calcium-rich foods (milk, yogurt), do weight-bearing exercises, get sunlight for vitamin D.

  19. Cancer (lifestyle-related types like lung, colon, breast)

    20. •Causes:
    Tobacco use, poor diet, alcohol consumption, radiation, genetics.

    •Symptoms:
    Unexplained lumps, weight loss, fatigue, bleeding.

    •Prevention:
    Avoid smoking, eat fiber-rich foods, limit alcohol, regular health checks.

  20. Depression

    21. •Causes:
    Chemical imbalance in brain, chronic stress, trauma, substance abuse.

    •Symptoms:
    Sadness, loss of interest, sleep disturbance, fatigue, suicidal thoughts.

    •Prevention:
    Regular exercise, social interaction, therapy, mindfulness, adequate rest.

  21. Obesity

    22. •Causes:
    Excess calorie intake, sedentary lifestyle, hormonal imbalance.

    •Symptoms:
    Excess body fat, breathlessness, fatigue, risk of diabetes and heart problems.

    •Prevention:
    Balanced diet, daily physical activity, portion control, and proper sleep.

  22. Heart Disease (Coronary Artery Disease)

    23. •Causes:
    Build-up of plaque in arteries due to high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, and poor diet.

    •Symptoms:
    Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeat.

    •Prevention:
    Eat heart-friendly foods (fruits, vegetables, whole grains), exercise, avoid tobacco, and maintain healthy blood pressure.

  23. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

    24. •Causes:
    High salt intake, stress, smoking, obesity, inactivity, and family history.

    •Symptoms:
    Often no symptoms; sometimes headaches, dizziness, or chest discomfort.

    •Prevention:
    Eat low-sodium meals, exercise daily, avoid alcohol and smoking, manage stress.

  24. Diabetes Mellitus (Type 2)

    25. •Causes:
    Insulin resistance due to obesity, lack of exercise, poor diet high in sugar and fats, and genetics.

    •Symptoms:
    Frequent urination, excessive thirst, blurred vision, fatigue, slow wound healing.

    •Prevention:
    Eat balanced meals, exercise regularly, maintain healthy weight, avoid sugary drinks, and check blood sugar levels.
